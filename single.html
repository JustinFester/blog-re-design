Article Title

Social media is integral to any good marketing strategy. In this day and age, businesses are expected to regularly interact across social channels to be considered visible online. If used strategically, social media is a powerful, fast and massively scalable tool to build your brand and establish relationships with your customer base. Posting Instagram photos can communicate a lifestyle brand, creating events on Facebook can increase exposure, and reaching out thought leaders on Twitter can get your business in the game.

But it’s not just about creating noise. Social media marketing takes strategy to build the right image and communicate effectively with your target audience. If abused, it can go quite wrong.

Take it from Trump.

His advisors took his Twitter privileges away after more than a few controversial statements and rants that didn’t go over so well with the American public. He shows us the danger and responsibility that comes with social media marketing – the image you portray can become viral, and can never be erased. So strategize, align your values with your message, and take it seriously to show your business in the best light.

As the 2016 election enters the home stretch this weekend, each candidate is working to make their final plea to the electorate by honing their respective messages. In a profile published by The New York Times, it seems that Donald Trump has relinquished the outlet for which he is best known, and which is most likely to land him in trouble: Twitter.
